Both these articles address the fundamental concern of whether the inventors of technology are responsible for the uses of their technology.  Facebook's challenge is to preserve the freedoms of speech we can all agree are fundamental to an informed discussion or electorate, while attempting to reduce the negative effects of mis-information or hate speech.  Both companies share the business model of collecting large amounts of user data with the interest of "targeting" content.  The article about the AmazonGo store also introduces the theme of whether or not automation will cost human jobs.

Since I have an optimistic view of technology, I tend towards the idea that the engineers are responsible for sending the rockets up, not where they land.  This having been said, I believe its incumbent upon the engineers to at least try to understand the ramifications of their work to the extent possible.
